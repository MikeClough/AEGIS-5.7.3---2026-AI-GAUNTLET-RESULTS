What 10/10 means on this radar

10/10 here does not mean “excellent in general.” It means the model scored near-perfectly on these weighted capability axes after every probe contributed across 2–3 dimensions.

Each radar point is now a weighted blend rather than a single category bucket. That makes the shape more stable as the library grows and makes each new probe influence multiple capability axes instead of distorting one silo.

Why “Creative” is emerging as a frontier weakness

The creative probes in this gauntlet are not mainly prose tests. They are constraint-audit tests: exact count limits, metaphor/simile distinctions, banned-token scans, and witness-style proof that the finished text really satisfies the rules.

A pattern is emerging in measured frontier runs because strong models tend to optimize for fluency, tone, and plausibility, then assert compliance instead of proving it. Gemini 3 and Grok 4.0 both lost most of their creative points to verification collapse rather than bad writing. On this rubric, polished prose without a checkable witness trail is not close to 10/10.
